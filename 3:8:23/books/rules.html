Danger Squares Example

Danger Squares

Danger Squares are the squares on the board where a piece can capture. It is illegal for the King to move into any of the opponent's danger squares as it would put him in check.

Check Example

Check

Check is when the King is put into one of the opponent's danger squares and he IS able to protect himself. When a King is put into check, he is forced to either move out of the danger square, put another one of his pieces in the attacker's way, or capture the attacker.

Checkmate Example

Checkmate

Checkmate is when the King is put into check but he IS NOT able to move to safety or block the attacker. This is one of the ways to win in chess.

Stalemate Example

Stalemate

Stalemate is when a player is unable to make any legal moves. This commonly happens when the player has no pieces left besides their King and the only places he can move is a Danger Square. This is one of the ways to draw in chess.

Repetition Example

Repetition

Repetition is when the same position is reached three times in a row. This is one of the ways to draw in chess.

Dead Position Example

Dead Position

A Dead Position is when both players don't have enough pieces to deliver checkmate to the other. This is one of the ways to draw in chess.

50 Move Rule Example

50 Move Rule

The 50 Move Rule is when 50 moves in a row are made in a game without a capture or a pawn movement. This is one of the ways to draw in chess.
